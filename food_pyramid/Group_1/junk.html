Junk Food

Back to food pyramid

What is considered as junk food?

Junk food is any food that contains high levels of fats, salt or sugar, and lacks nutrients such as fibre, vitamins and minerals.

Some examples of junk food include:

  • Cakes and biscuits
  • Fast foods (burgers and pizzas)
  • Chocolates and other sweets
  • Processed meats (bacon)
  • Snacks (crisps)
  • Sugary drinks
  • Alcoholic drinks

How does junk food affect your body?

While it is acceptable to have these kinds of foods so long as it is kept to a minimum, a diet that is high in fats, sugar and sodium cause long-lasting damage to your body. Constantly having unhealthy foods while not receiving enough of the proper nutrients can increase the risk of obesity and other chronic diseases.

These diseases may include:

  • Cardiovascular disease
  • Type-2 diabetes
  • Non-alcoholic fatty liver disease
  • Certain cancers
