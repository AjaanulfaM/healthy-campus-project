What are considered as fruits?

In a botanical sense, a fruit is the fleshy or dry ripened ovary of a flowering plant, enclosing the seed or seeds. Apricots, bananas, and grapes, as well as bean pods, corn grains, tomatoes, cucumbers, and (in their shells) acorns and almonds, are all technically fruits..

Other examples of fruits include:

  • Apples
  • Kiwis
  • Mangoes
  • Coconuts
  • Peaches
  • Blueberries

Benefits of including fruits to your diet.

A diet rich in both vegetables and fruits can lower blood pressure, reduce the risk of heart disease and stroke, prevent some types of cancer, lower risk of eye and digestive problems, and have a positive effect upon blood sugar, which can help keep appetite in check. Eating non-starchy fruits like apples and pears may even promote weight loss. [1] Their low glycemic loads prevent blood sugar spikes that can increase hunger.
